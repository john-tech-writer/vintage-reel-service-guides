# Reel Stories: The Reel that Started It All
My First Reel

This is one of the earlier-model Zebco 33 reels, an American spincast reel that probably introduced millions of kids to fishing.

But this isn’t just any Zeb 33. This was my reel when I was a kid in the 60s. It was in a box of stuff, stored away, more or less forgotten, and hadn’t worked in a long time. A few months ago I got it out of storage and determined to either fix it and use it or let it go. It’s working now, and one thing, one reel, led to another.

# Reelin’ in the Years

Retirement . . . re-evaluation . . . of my life direction, taking stock, and thinking about what to do for the rest of my life since I didn’t have to work anymore . . . going through all the stuff I’d accumulated, in my garage and in my head, clarifying and making space for what I really love. I started discovering something I’d forgotten.

In all that busy life-stuff—working, raising a family, keeping the house and garden neat—I’d set aside a few things, and fishing was one of them. I didn’t realize that until I started working on the old reels and rods left over from my childhood . . . I started connecting with things long forgotten, things worth remembering . . . fishing with Dad . . . “of all the things to keep,” I thought, “to drag around with me for 50-plus years . . .” These must have seemed important, some tangible reminder of innocent fun times, the excitement of catching a fish, all that went with it.

eBay, spare parts, Perplexity, GitHub, and the technical writing that wouldn’t go away.
