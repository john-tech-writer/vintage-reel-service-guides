# Substack series titles and tags

Substack is the *notification hub and narrative center* of the overall stack. Notifications for all new repo overviews and guides, and accompanying YouTube videos, are published here.

Initially there are four Substack series for the VRSG substack. As new series are developed they should be documented here. Each series gets a title and a tag:

- The series title is the lead text for each post title. The pattern is `Welcome to the Bench: The [maker] [model]`, for example, *Welcome to the Bench: The Pflueger Nobby*. These are more creative and literary than the tags.

- The tag is assigned to the post "behind the scenes" and serves to group posts so that site visitors can easily see logical groupings using clear terms. For example, Welcome to the Bench posts are tagged *new arrival*.

Here are all four initial series titles and their tags:

- Welcome to the bench: When a new reel comes to the bench, a bit about why and how it was acquired and what the plans are for it. **Tag = New Arrival**

- At the bench: May be changed to something like "Lights, camera, action" or "Lights, camera, reel me in." Production notes on how the images and videos are made. **Tag = Production Notes**

- Service guides: When a new overview + guide + video stack is completely published, a notification. **Tag = Guides**

- Reel stories: When service is completed on a reel, a story about taking it into the field, how it performs, where it is integrated with other tackle to make a balanced outfit, what fish are caught; also stories about how a reel is significant in my life, for example, the hero post for substack, about my first reel. **Tag = Field Stories**

The hero post is tagged "Field Stories."

## Other ideas for series

- Reel Zen: Notes on the meditative aspects of bench work, casting practice, and fishing. Probably would use existing tags like Production Notes, Field Stories, depending on the content. So some series titles might use multiple tags, and probably vice versa.
